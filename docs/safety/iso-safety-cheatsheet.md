# ISO 24409 / ISO 7010 Safety Sign Cheat Sheet (Maritime/Subsea)

## Color and shape rules
- Prohibition: red ring/diagonal on white with black pictogram; circular.
- Fire equipment: red square/rectangle with white pictogram.
- Warning: yellow triangle with black border/pictogram.
- Mandatory: blue circle with white pictogram.
- Safe condition: green rectangle/square with white pictogram.
- Stripes: black/yellow at 45 degrees for hazards; red/white for fire equipment zones.

## Common pictograms (ID and description)
- Prohibition: P001 no smoking; P002 no open flame; P003 no access for unauthorized; P010 no lifting; P012 no metal tools.
- Fire equipment: F001 fire extinguisher; F002 fire hose reel; F004 fire alarm call point; F005 fire telephone; F006 fire blanket.
- Warning: W001 general warning; W002 explosive atmosphere; W003 flammable material; W004 electric shock; W008 corrosive material; W012 industrial vehicles; W018 overhead load; W021 slippery surface.
- Mandatory (PPE/actions): M001 general mandatory; M004 wear eye protection; M006 wear hearing protection; M013 wear respiratory protection; M016 wear face shield; M017 wear lifejacket; M018 wear gloves; M025 wear safety harness; M027 wear protective clothing.
- Safe condition: E001 emergency exit left; E002 emergency exit right; E003 first aid; E004 emergency eyewash; E005 emergency shower; E007 assembly point; E009 emergency telephone.

## Usage notes
- Match symbol IDs to ISO 24409 (shipboard) or ISO 7010 equivalents; keep consistent across vessels/assets.
- Pair symbols with brief text: hazard/requirement, consequence, action.
- Maintain visibility: adequate size, contrast, and lighting; avoid obstructions.
- Place fire and emergency signs at eye level and along access routes; repeat where direction changes.
